TITLE: Immigration Security and Border Integrity Act 2026

ACTNUMBER: 2026 No 02

STATUS: in force

TYPE: Act

VERSION: latest

AGENCY: Ministry of Immigration & Customs

DATEOFASSENT: 9 March 2026

COMMENCEMENT: see section 2

PARTNUMBER: 1

PARTTITLE: Preliminary Provisions

LONGTITLE: An Act to strengthen the security and integrity of Square Country’s borders; to ensure that all persons entering the country are properly identified and screened; to improve monitoring, enforcement, and compliance within the immigration system; and to provide for related matters.

SECTION: 1

HEADING: Short Title

This Act may be cited as the **Immigration Security and Border Integrity Act 2026**.

SECTION: 2

HEADING: Commencement

1. This Act shall come into force on a date appointed by Order in Council made by the Governor-General on the advice of the Minister of Immigration & Customs.
2. Different dates may be appointed for different Parts of Provisions of this Act.

SECTION: 3

HEADING: Purpose

The Purpose of this Act is to—
(a) strengthen border security and monitoring systems;
(b) ensure that all persons entering Square Country are properly identified and screened;
(c) prevent unlawful entry and immigration fraud;
(d) promote lawful, controlled, and transparent immigration processes;
(e) protect national security, economic stability, and public safety; and
(f) ensure that immigration policy serves the national interest of Square Country.

SECTION: 4

HEADING: Interpretation

In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—
**“Authority”** means the Border Security Authority established under section 8.
**“Border monitoring system”** means any electronic, biometric, or surveillance system used to monitor entry and exit from Square Country.
**“Director”** means the Director of Border Security appointed under section 10.
**“Immigration officer”** means any officer authorised to enforce immigration laws at ports of entry or within the territory of Square Country.
**“Minister”** means the Minister of Immigration & Customs.
**“Port of entry”** means any airport, seaport, or land border crossing designated by regulation for lawful entry into Square Country.
**“Unlawful entrant”** means a person who enters or remains in Square Country without lawful authority.

SECTION: 5

HEADING: Binding Effect

This Act binds the Crown in right of Square Country.

SECTION: 6

HEADING: Act to be Read with Other Enactments

This act shall be read together with all other immigration, customs, and national security enactments, except where inconsistent.

SECTION: 7

HEADING: Principles of Immigration Administration

All persons exercising powers or functions under this Act shall be guided by the following principles—
(a) protection of national sovereignty and border integrity;
(b) strict enforcement of lawful immigration procedures;
(c) fairness and consistency in application of immigration laws;
(d) transparency and accountability in immigration administration; and
(e) prioritisation of national security and public safety.

PARTNUMBER: 2

PARTTITLE: Border Security and Administration

SECTION: 8

HEADING: Establishment of the Borde Securty Authority

1. There is hereby established an authority to be known as the Border Security Authority (hereafter referred to as the “Authority”).
2. The Authority shall be responsible for monitoring entry and exit at all national borders.
3. The Authority shall operate as a department of state with powers necessary to enforce immigration and border laws.
4. The Authority is a body corporate with perpetual succession, a common seal, and may sue or be sued in its corporate name.

SECTION: 9

HEADING: Functions of the Authority

The functions of the Authority are to—
(a) monitor and control entry into Square Country;
(b) administer screening procedures for all travellers;
(c) oversee border surveillance systems and technology
(d) coordinate with intelligence and security agencies;
(e) detect and prevent unlawful entry and trafficking; and
(f) enforce compliance with immigration laws.

SECTION: 10

HEADING: Director of Border Security

1. There shall be a Director of Border Security, who shall be the chief executive of the Authority.
2. The Director shall be appointed by the Governor-General on the advice of the Minister.
3. The Director shall hold office for a term not exceeding ten years and may be reappointed.
4. The Director shall oversee the enforcement of all provisions of this Act.

SECTION: 11

HEADING: Deputies and Other Officers

1. The Director may appoint deputy directors and immigration officers as necessary.
2. Officers shall carry out duties relating to inspections, enforcement, monitoring, and immigration compliance.

SECTION: 12

HEADING: Coordination with Security Agencies

The Authority shall coordinate with national security, intelligence, and law enforcement agencies to identify risks related to unlawful entry, organised crime, or threats to national security.

SECTION: 13

HEADING: Border Monitoring Systems

1. The Authority shall implement modern border monitoring systems including—
(a) biometric identification systems;
(b) automated entry and exit records;
(c) surveillance technology at ports of entry; and
(d) integrated databases for immigration enforcement.
2. These systems shall be used to track entry, departure, and immigration status.

SECTION: 14

HEADING: Entry Screening Requirements

1. Every person entering Square Country must undergo identity verification and security screening.
2. Screening may include—
(a) passport and visa verification;
(b) biometric identification;
(c) background checks against security databases; and
(d) customs and immigration interviews.

SECTION: 15

HEADING: Ports of Entry and Inspection Facilities

1. Entry into Square Country shall occur only through designated ports of entry.
2. Inspection facilities shall be established at each port of entry to conduct security and immigration checks.

PARTNUMBER: 3

PARTTITLE: Immigration Control and Enforcement

SECTION: 16

HEADING: Mandatory Identity Verification

1. All foreign nationals entering Square Country must present valid travel documents and identification.
2. No person shall be admitted without successful verification of identity.

SECTION: 17

HEADING: Visa Integrity Measures

1. The Minister may establish visa requirements and conditions to regulate entry.
2. Immigration officers may deny entry where there is reasonable suspicion of fraud, misrepresentation, or security risk.

SECTION: 18

HEADING: Detention

1. Immigration officers may detain individuals suspected of unlawful entry pending verification of identity and immigration status.
2. Detention shall be subject to procedures prescribed by regulation.

SECTION: 19

HEADING: Removal and Deportation Procedures

1. Any person determined to be unlawfully present may be removed from Square Country.
2. Removal orders may be issued by authorised immigration officials in accordance with prescribed procedures.

SECTION: 20

HEADING: Employer Compliance and Work Verification

1. Employers must verify the legal work status of all foreign employees.
2. Employers who knowingly employ unlawful entrants commit an offence.

SECTION: 21

HEADING: Penalties for Immigration Fraud

Any person who knowingly—
(a) provides false immigration documentation;
(b) Assists unlawful entry; or
(c) Engages in visa fraud—
commits an offence and may be liable to penalties prescribed by regulation.

SECTION: 22

HEADING: Temporary Entry Quotas and Controls

The Minister may establish quotas or limits on temporary entry categories to ensure that immigration levels remain consistent with national economic and security interests.

PARTNUMBER: 4

PARTTITLE: Oversight and Reporting

SECTION: 23

HEADING: Auditor-General Oversight

The Auditor-General shall review expenditure and operational effectiveness of the Border Security Authority.

SECTION: 24

HEADING: Parliamentary Reporting

The Minister shall present and annual report to Parliament outlining—
(a) immigration statistics;
(b) enforcement activities;
(c) border security developments; and
(d) compliance outcomes.

SECTION: 25

HEADING: Transparency and Recordkeeping

All immigration officers shall maintain records of enforcement activities and decisions to ensure transparency and accountability.

PARTNUMBER: 5

PARTTITLE: Miscellaneous and Final Provisions

SECTION: 26

HEADING: Regulations

The Minister may make regulations for—
(a) border monitoring and screening procedures;
(b) immigration enforcement and compliance;
(c) Visa requirements and conditions; and
(d) Penalties for offences under this Act.

SECTION: 27

HEADING: Protection from Liability

No action shall lie against the Minister, Director, or officers for any act done in good faith under this act.

SECTION: 28

HEADING: Review of Operation

The Minister shall cause a review of the operation of this Act five years after commencement to assess its effectiveness in maintaining secure borders and lawful immigration.

SECTION: 29

HEADING: Repeal of previous enactments

1. The following enactments are repealed:
(a) the Immigrations (Open Arms) Act 2006;
(b) any other enactment inconsistent with this Act.
2. Regulations made under repealed enactments continue in force until replaced.
