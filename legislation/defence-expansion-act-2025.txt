Defence Expansion Act 2025
________________




TITLE: Defence Expansion Act 2025


STATUS: In Force


VERSION: 30 November 2025


ADMINISTERED: Ministry of Defence


PART: Preliminary Provisions


An act to strengthen the national defence of Square Country; to increase funding, modernise capabilities, and improve efficiency in the management and oversight of the armed forces; and to provide for related matters.


SECTION: 1


HEADING: Short Title


This Act may be cited as the Defence Expansion Act 2025.


SECTION: 2


HEADING: Commencement
        
1. This Act shall come into force on a date appointed by Order in Council made by the Governor-General on the advice of the Minister of Defence.
2. Different dates may be appointed for different Parts or provisions of this Act.


SECTION: 3


HEADING: Purpose
        
The purpose of this Act is to—
1. increase funding allocated to defence to ensure national security;
2. Modernise the armed forces through acquisition of advanced equipment, infrastructure, and technology;
3. Enhance the readiness, capability, and efficiency of the military;
4. Ensure transparency, accountability, and proper oversight in defence expenditure; and
5. Promote the welfare and training of military personnel.


SECTION: 4


HEADING: Interpretation
        
In this Act, unless context otherwise requires—
“Armed Forces” means the Army, Navy, Air Force, and any other service established under law.
“Commissioner” means the Chief of Defence Administration appointed under section 10.
“Department” means the Department Of Defence.
“Minister” means the Minister of Defence.
“Funding” means all appropriated sums allocated for defence purposes.
“Officers” means persons employed by the Ministry of Defence in the performance of its
functions.


SECTION: 5


HEADING: Binding Effect
        
This Act binds the Crown in right of Square Country.


SECTION: 6


HEADING: Act to be Read with Other Enactments
        
This Act shall be read together with all other defence-related enactments, except where
inconsistent.


SECTION: 7


HEADING: Principles of Defence Administration
        
All persons exercising powers or functions under this Act shall be guided by the following
principles—
1. Efficiency and effectiveness in use of defence resources;
2. Transparency and accountability in expenditure;
3. Readiness and preparedness for national security needs;
4. Welfare and fair treatment of military personnel; and
5. Strategic modernisation and capability development.


PART: Establishment and Administration


SECTION: 8


HEADING: Establishment of the Department of Defence
        
1. There is hereby established a department of state to be known as the Department of Defence.
2. The Department shall be responsible for the administration of defence forces, expenditure, and policies.
3. The Department shall be a body corporate with perpetual succession, a common seal, and power to sue and be sued in its corporate name.


SECTION: 9


HEADING: Functions of the Department
        
The functions of the Department are to—
1. manage and allocate defence funding;
2. oversee procurement, maintenance, and modernisation of equipment;
3. ensure operational readiness of all branches of the armed forces;
4. provide advice to the Prime Minister and Cabinet on defence policy and strategic planning;
5. maintain training, welfare, and education programs for military personnel;
6. audit, monitor, and report on the use of defence resources; and
7. perform any other function conferred by this or any other enactment.


SECTION: 10


HEADING: Chief of Defence Administration


1. There shall be a Chief of Defence Administration, who shall be the chief executive of the Department.
2. The Chief shall be appointed by the Governor-General on the advice of the Minister of Defence.
3. The Chief shall hold office for a term not exceeding seven years and may be reappointed.
4. The Chief shall act independently in managing defence funds and operations.


SECTION: 11


HEADING: Deputies and Other Officers        


1. The Chief may appoint Deputy Chiefs and other officers as required.
2. Officers shall perform duties as delegated and take oaths of fidelity and secrecy.


SECTION: 12


HEADING: Independence and Accountability
        
1. The Department shall operate independently and free from political interference in day-to-day operational decisions.
2. Oversight shall be provided by the Auditor-General and the Parliamentary Defence Committee


SECTION: 13


HEADING: Divisions of the Department
        
1. The Department shall include, but is not limited to—
(a) Operations and Strategy Division;
(b) Procurement and Modernisation Division;
(c) Personnel and Welfare Division;
(d) Finance and Budget Division;
(e) Intelligence and Cybersecurity Division; and
(f) Legal and Compliance Division.


SECTION: 14


HEADING: Advisory Committee
        
The Minister may establish advisory committees to advise on defence strategy, technology, and
expenditure efficiency.


SECTION: 15


HEADING: Annual Reports
        
1. The Chief shall submit annual reports on defence funding usage, modernisation progress, and readiness levels.
2. Reports shall be laid before Parliament.
PART: Defence Funding Framework


SECTION: 16


HEADING: Funding Allocation
        
1. Parliament shall allocate increased funding to the Department of Defence annually to ensure operational readiness and modernisation.
2. The Department may redistribute funds internally among divisions to meet strategic priorities.


SECTION: 17


HEADING: Expenditure Principles
        
All defence expenditure shall follow principles of—
1. Transparency;
2. Efficiency;
3. Strategic necessity;
4. Personnel welfare; and
5. Accountability.


SECTION: 18


HEADING: Procurement and Modernisation
        
1. The Department shall modernise equipment and infrastructure according to prescribed regulations.
2. Procurement contracts shall be awarded in accordance with principles of fairness and competitiveness.


SECTION: 19


HEADING: Operational Readiness
        
The Department shall ensure that the Armed Forces maintain readiness levels consistent with
national security requirements, including personnel training, maintenance of equipment, and
strategic deployments.


PART: Oversight and Reporting


SECTION: 20


HEADING: Auditor-General Oversight
         
The Auditor-General shall audit all defence expenditure and report to Parliament annually.


SECTION: 21


HEADING: Parliamentary Reporting
        
The Minister shall submit reports to Parliament on—
1. funding allocations and expenditures;
2. modernisation and procurement progress; and
3. readiness and capability metrics.


SECTION: 22


HEADING: Transparency and Accountability
        
All officers shall maintain records and provide documentation to ensure public accountability of
defence funds.


PART: Miscellaneous and Final Provisions


SECTION: 23


HEADING: Regulations
        
The Minister may, on recommendation of the Chief, make regulations for—
1. Allocation and management of funds;
2. Procurement and acquisition
3. Personnel and training standards; and
4. Operational procedures for readiness.


SECTION: 24


HEADING: Protection from Liability
        
No action shall lie against the Minister, Chief, or officers for any act done in good faith under this         Act.


SECTION: 25


HEADING: Review of Operation
        
The Minister shall cause a review of the operation of this Act five years after commencement to
assess effectiveness, efficiency, and fiscal responsibility.
