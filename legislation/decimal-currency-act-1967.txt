TITLE: Decimal Currency Act 1967

ACTNUMBER: 1967 No 29

STATUS: In Force

VERSION: Latest

AGENCY: Ministry of Finance

DATEOFASSENT: 31 May 1967

COMMENCEMENT: see section 2

PARTNUMBER: 1

PARTTITLE: Preliminary Provisions

An Act to provide for the introduction of decimal currency in Square Country, to establish the unit of account known as the Square Dollar, to make provision for the conversion of existing currency, and for matters incidental thereto.

SECTION: 1

HEADING: Short Title

1. This Act may be cited as the **Decimal Currency Act 1967**.



SECTION: 2


HEADING: Commencement

1. This Act shall come into force on the date of its assent, except as provided in subsection (2).
2. Parts 1 and 2 shall come into force on **Decimal Day**, being the day appointed by Order in Council under section 9.

SECTION: 3

HEADING: Purpose

The purpose of this Act is—
1. to establish a decimal system of currency for Square Country; and
2. To provide for the orderly conversion of all monetary expressions; and
3. to ensure continuity of contract, obligations, and public administration during the transition.

SECTION: 4

HEADING: Interpretation

In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires—
**“Bank”** means any institution authorised to accept deposits.
**“Decimal Day”** means the day appointed under Section 9.
**“Minister”** means the Minister of Finance.
**“Pre-decimal currency”** means the pound, shilling, and penny system in force before Decimal Day.
**“Square Dollar”** means the unit of currency established under section 6.

SECTION: 5

HEADING: Act binds the Crown

This Act binds the Crown.

PARTNUMBER: 2

PARTTITLE: Establishment of Decimal Currency System

SECTION: 6

HEADING: Square Dollar to be unit of currency

1. The monetary unit of Square Country shall be the Square Dollar.
2. The symbol for the Square Dollar shall be ₴.

SECTION: 7

HEADING: Subdivision of the Square Dollar

1. The Square Dollar shall be divided into 100 cents.
2. The symbol for cent shall be c.

SECTION: 8

HEADING: Legal tender status

1. Decimal notes and coins issued under this Act shall be legal tender.
2. Pre-decimal currency shall remain legal tender only during the transitional period specified in section 10.

SECTION: 9

HEADING: Decimal Day

1. The Governor-General may by Order in Council, appoint a day to be known as Decimal Day, being the day on which the decimal system established by this Act shall take effect.
2. This day shall be the **14th day of August, 1967**.

SECTION: 10

HEADING: Transitional dual-currency period

1. For a period of six months after Decimal Day, both decimal and pre-decimal currency shall be legal tender.
2. After that period, pre-decimal currency shall cease to be legal tender by may be redeemed under section 22.

PARTNUMBER: 3

PARTTITLE: Conversion of Existing Currency

SECTION: 11

HEADING: Conversion rate

1. The conversion rate shall be ₴2 = £1.
2. All monetary expressions shall be converted in accordance with Schedule 1.

SECTION: 12

HEADING: Rounding rules

1. Amounts shall be rounded to the nearest cent.
2. Where the amount is exactly halfway, it shall be rounded upward.

SECTION: 13

HEADING: Conversion of contracts

1. All contracts, agreements, and instruments expressed in pre-decimal currency shall, on and after Decimal Day, be read as if expressed in decimal currency.
2. No contract shall be invalidated by reason only of conversion.

SECTION: 14

HEADING: Conversion of wages, salaries, and pensions

All wages, salaries, pensions, and similar payments shall be converted in accordance with the conversion rate and rounding rules.

SECTION: 15

HEADING: Conversion of taxes, duties, and fees

All taxes, duties, levies, and fees payable to the Crown shall be converted on Decimal Day.

SECTION: 16

HEADING: Conversion of bank accounts

1. All bank accounts shall be converted automatically on Decimal Day.
2. Banks shall provide statements showing pre- and post- conversion balances.

SECTION: 17

HEADING: Conversion of securities and government bonds

All securities, bonds, and financial instruments issued by the Crown or any public authority shall be converted in accordance with this act.

PARTNUMBER: 4

PARTTITLE: Issue, Design, and Withdrawal of Currency

SECTION: 18

HEADING: Authority to issue decimal notes and coin

The Central Bank of Square Country may issue decimal notes and coins

SECTION: 19

HEADING: Denominations

1. Notes may be issued in denominations of ₴5, ₴10, ₴20, ₴50, ₴100, and such other denominations as may be prescribed.
2. Coins may be issued in denominations of 5c, 10c, 20c, 50c, ₴1, ₴2.

SECTION: 20

HEADING: Design approval

1. The design of all notes and coins shall be approved by the Minister.

SECTION: 21

HEADING: Withdrawal of pre-decimal currency

The Central Bank may withdraw pre-decimal notes and coins from circulation.

SECTION: 22

HEADING: Redemption of withdrawn currency

Withdrawn currency may be redeemed at its decimal equivalent for a period of ten years after Decimal Day.

PARTNUMBER: 5

PARTTITLE: Accounting, Pricing, and Public Administration

SECTION: 23

HEADING: Decimal accounting requirements

All accounts kept by public bodies, companies, and partnerships shall be kept in decimal currency on or after Decimal Day.

SECTION: 24

HEADING: Pricing in decimal currency

1. All goods and services shall be pricked in decimal currency on and after Decimal Day.

SECTION: 25

HEADING: Public sector conversion obligations

Government departments shall ensure that all forms, notices, and publications are updated to reflect decimal currency.

SECTION: 26

HEADING: Banking sector obligations

Banks shall—
1. provide public information on conversion;
2. ensure systems are capable of decimal accounting;
3. comply with any directions issued by the Minister.

SECTION: 27

HEADING: Public information campaign

The Minister shall ensure that a comprehensive public information campaign is conducted before Decimal Day.

PARTNUMBER: 6

PARTTITLE: Offences and Enforcement

SECTION: 28

HEADING: Misrepresentation of conversion rates

A person commits an offence who knowingly misrepresents the conversion rate.

SECTION: 29

HEADING: Refusal to accept legal tender

A person commits an offence who refuses to accept decimal currency during the transitional period. 

SECTION: 30

HEADING: False or misleading pricing

A person commits an offence who displays prices in a manner likely to mislead the public during the transition.

SECTION: 31

HEADING: Powers of inspectors

Inspectors appointed under this Act may enter premises, examine records, and require information for the purpose of enforcing this Act.

PARTNUMBER: 7

PARTTITLE: Regulation-Making Powers

SECTION: 32

HEADING: Regulations

The Governor-General may take regulations for the purpose of carrying this Act into effect.

SECTION: 33

HEADING: Ministerial notices

The Minister may issue notices prescribing matters of detail relating to conversion, pricing, and public information.

PARTNUMBER: 9

PARTTITLE: Miscellaneous

SECTION: 34

HEADING: Repeals of previous enactments

The following enactments are repealed:
(a) the Legal Tender Act 1927;
(b) any other enactment inconsistent with this Act.
1. Regulations made under repealed enactments continue in force until replaced.
